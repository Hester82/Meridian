Information

When you move into your new home you will be given a Resident Move-In Checklist and a Parking Registration. Please fill these out completely and return to the office as soon as possible. The Checklist is for you to note any pre-existing damages in the apartment. By noting these, we can promptly fix these problems. The Parking Registration is used to track which buildings may be having parking problems.

Once you settle in, should a maintenance problem occur, please call the office immediately so that we may service your request as quickly as possible. As stated in your lease, it is the leaseholder’s responsibility to notify the office of any service requests that you have. Should something occur after office hours you may call the office and our answering service will be happy to direct your call. You may submit an online maintenance request form by visiting our website at www.meridianpark.net. You can also fill out a maintenance request form found on the bulletin board in the clubhouse or in the fitness room and place it in our convenient drop box.

We issue parking permits to our residents and ask that you read the parking guidelines included in your move in package and call the office with any questions you have regarding these guidelines.

Meridian Park publishes and delivers a monthly newsletter that notifies our residents of any community events and reminders you should be aware of. It is packed with useful information that is beneficial while you stay at Meridian Park and in Greenville.

Everyone is looking for a way to make his or her life easier. One way is to sign up for the Tenant Portal. Instead of dropping off a check every month, you just log on to your account and pay online. You can even put in a non-emergency work order. For those that are not quite ready to pay your rent online, we have a late night drop box located in the front of the clubhouse where you can drop off your rent, keys, or letters of correspondence.

Clean air is a must and in order to keep your air conditioner and heater in good working condition, it’s been said to change your air filters every 1-3 months depending on if you have allergies, pets or even on the season. So as a courtesy we will change your air filter twice a year. We will give you adequate notice via our newsletter so that you will know approximately which week someone will be entering your apartment. As an additional courtesy you may call the office and we will bring you air filters as needed.

When operating the garbage disposal in your apartment, please remember to run the warm water. This will prevent the food from jamming the system. Please DO NOT put grease, bones, or any other hard objects in the disposal, if you can’t chew it neither can the disposal! There will be a $45 service charge for any maintenance calls regarding improper use of garbage disposals. Should the disposal give off an unpleasant odor lemon juice or baking soda will freshen it right up! Also make sure the disposal is clear when running your dishwasher, this will prevent food from the disposal entering your washer while in process.

To increase the efficiency of your dryer and cut down on your utility bill, make sure to clean the lint filter after every load. The build up of lint will force the dryer to work harder!

To reduce the maintenance problems that may arise in your bathroom, we ask that you not flush feminine products, paper towels, or cat litter down the toilet. If you do not have a plunger please purchase one prior to calling the office. If we are able to unclog the line or we have to call a plumber to unclog the line and it because of these products, you will be charged $45 and up, pending service fees.

Rules

Residents will keep balcony and patios neat and clean at all times and not store, hang, drape rugs, towels, laundry, wash or other household items on the railings or other portions of balconies or patios, Please do not hang towels, flags, banners, clothing etc. from outside railing. No hardware is allowed to penetrate the exterior materials of the building. Residents will respect the rights of others, exercising due care while sweeping dirt, rubbish, trash or other items to make sure that such does not fall onto the property or ground below, or in any way annoy occupants of other apartments. We take pride in our community and would like everyone to help in keeping up the appearance of the property.

Being respectful of others is very important. Radios, stereos, TV’s, and pets should not be heard louder than normal conversational level in another unit with the unit doors and windows open. Patio usage also should be monitored so the noise level does not exceed this limit. Vehicle noise, including stereos, and engine or muffler noise should be kept to a minimum and not become a nuisance. All noise should be kept to a minimum between 10:00 pm and 8:00 am. No obnoxious or offensive activity that may become an annoyance or nuisance in the neighborhood is allowed.

Breezeways are to be kept clear of debris and clutter. Any furniture should be limited to that which can fit in front of the unit’s window. There must be 3ft of clearance between any object (furniture, plants, etc.) and the edge of the breezeway. The edge of the breezeway is defined as the railing on the second floor and where similar railing would exist on the first floor. Furniture must be designed for outdoor use, in good condition and be in compliance with all city ordinances. Bikes are not to be parked in breezeways, walkways. You may keep them on your patio or balcony, but we advise that you lock it up.

If you need to park your boat or trailer on the property please contact the office so that you can park them in appropriate spaces.

Mold and Mildew

Mold and Mildew: Resident acknowledges and agrees that, for the maintenance of the premises and for the health and well-being of residents and resident’s guests, it is necessary for the resident to provide appropriate climate control, keep premises clean, and take necessary measures to retard and prevent mold and mildew from accumulating in the premises.

Residents agree to:

  • 1. Clean and dust the apartment on a regular basis and to remove any visible moisture that has accumulated on windows, walls, and other surfaces as soon as such accumulation is visibly apparent.
  • 2. Immediately notify Meridian Park Office Staff of any evidence of water leaks or excessive moisture or standing water inside the apartment.
  • 3. Immediately notify Meridian Park Office Staff of any mold, mildew or similar growth in the apartment that persists after Resident has attempted to remove it through the application of common household cleaning solutions or anti-bacterial products.
  • 4. Immediately notify Meridian Park Office Staff of any heating, air-conditioning, ventilation, plumbing or washer and dryer malfunctions.
  • 5. Immediately notify Meridian Park Office Staff of any inoperable doors or windows in the apartment.
  • 6. Make sure you operate the heating and air-conditioning system in a manner necessary to properly ventilate the apartment to prevent an environment conducive to the growth of mold and mildew, including change the air filter every 30 days.
  • 7. Operate the stove and shower exhaust fans in a manner to properly ventilate the apartment during cooking and showering so as to prevent and environment conducive to the growth of mold and mildew.
    Resident agrees that resident shall be solely responsible for damages caused to the premises and to personal property present on the premises as well as any injuries or adverse medical condition suffered by resident or guests resulting from resident’s failure to comply with the terms of this paragraph.

Fungi and Bacteria Liability Exclusion

Tenant acknowledges and agrees that the Landlord or Landlord’s Agent is not liable for injury, damage or expenses resulting from “fungi” or bacteria. “Fungi” means any type or form of fungus, including mold, or mildew and any mycotoxins, spores, scents or by products produced or released by fungi.

Injury, damage or expenses includes “bodily injury”, “property damage” or “personal and adverting injury” which would not have occurred, in whole or in part, but for the actual, alleged or threatened inhalation of, ingestion of, contact with, exposure to, existence of, or presence of, any “fungi” or bacteria on or within a building or structure, including its contents, regardless of whether any other cause, event, material or product contributed concurrently or in any sequence to such injury or damage.

Fire Safety Tips

Living Room Fire Safety

  • * Keep household combustibles away from any heating source.
  • * Never overload electrical outlets. Keep electrical cords out of harms way.
  • * Never override or bypass fuses or circuit breakers.
  • * Replace any electrical cord that is cracked or frayed.
  • * Place large, deep, noncombustible ashtrays on safe surfaces.
  • * Douse ashtrays with water before discarding embers.
  • * Always keep matches away from children.

Kitchen Fire Safety

  • * NEVER leave food unattended while cooking.
  • * Keep stove tops and ovens clean to avoid grease fires.
  • * Always cook with pot handles away from front edge of stove.
  • * Clean toaster oven of crumbs.
  • * Keep combustibles such as towels, potholders and food packages away from stove.

We ask that you call 911 1st when there is a problem then call the Office to notify Management of the situation.
